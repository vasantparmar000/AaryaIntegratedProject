What is the work of GSAT?

GSAT or Geosynchronous Satellite, is a series of satellites that provide communication services for India.

Purpose

GSAT satellites support telecommunications services for the Indian subcontinent, including television broadcasting, internet, weather forecasting, disaster warning and etc.

1. Telecommunication: A telecommunication satellite is an artificial satellite that relays and amplifies radio telecommunication signals via a transponder; it creates a communication channel between a source transmitter and a receiver at different locations on the earth.

Television broadcasting: it is the distribution of audiovisual content to viewers through the airwaves, cables, or satellites. it can also refers to the telecommunications networks that distributes television content.

Internet: it is a satellite-based internet service that is a part of Geo Stationary Satellite {GSAT} series of communication satellites.

Weather forecasting: Weather forecasts use a variety of methods to predict the state of the atmosphere at a given time and place, including satellite data, computer models, and human input.

Disaster Warning: most of these satellites contain passive or active sensors that can be utilized by the scientific community for the remote sensing of natural hazards. passive sensors record reflected [visible and infrared wavelengths] and emitted energy [thermal wavelengths]. THANKS
